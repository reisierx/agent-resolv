The AI-native insurance and credit broker for Portugal's underserved SME market

74% of Portuguese SMEs carry inadequate coverage. The traditional intermediary network has collapsed 37% since 2019. Credit intermediation exploded from 9% to 57% of mortgage volume in five years, but 93.9% of intermediaries are non-compliant. Fewer than 1% of insurance premiums are sold online. The market is broken — and structurally ready.

agent-resolv inverts the industry. We start with the customer's actual needs, not product catalogs. We hold dual ASF corretor de seguros and BdP credit intermediary licenses — fully independent advice across insurance and lending. The AI agent handles omni-channel intake (WhatsApp, Telegram, email, voice, API), builds a persistent customer account, routes to all providers independently, and returns a recommendation. Humans hold regulatory accountability.

IDD consolidation eliminating weak intermediaries. FiDA (2027–28) standardising insurance data access. Mortgage boom driving cross-sell demand. AI capability threshold crossed. Digital channel infrastructure production-ready. A 18–36 month window.

01 The Problem

The industry is built backwards

Suppliers design pre-packaged products. Brokers present what earns the most commission. Customers receive 80-page PDFs designed for compliance, not clarity. They are left to decipher, compare, and hope they chose correctly. The customer's actual needs — what they want to protect, how much coverage, at what price — are an afterthought.

88% of SMEs cannot explain what their professional liability insurance covers — the highest rate in Europe. One in three SME owners has not reviewed their policies in over three years. 55% lack basic general liability or property insurance. 54% suffered a cyberattack in the past year yet fewer than 15% carry cyber insurance.

The intermediary network is collapsing. Registered intermediaries fell 37% from 16,783 (2019) to 10,489 (2023). Surviving individual agents average just €11,717/year in commissions — below the national minimum wage. The predicted trajectory is a further halving.

Fewer than 1% of insurance premiums are sold online. Only 4% of Portuguese consumers have purchased insurance through a comparison site (vs. 11% EU-wide). ASF received 28,939 formal complaints in 2024.

Credit intermediation is scaling but chaotic. Mortgage intermediation exploded from 9% to 57% of all mortgage volume in five years. But Banco de Portugal found a 93.9% irregularity rate among credit intermediaries. The country has just 68 licensed independent brokers across a €14.3 billion premium market serving 1.1 million SMEs. That is a structural vacuum.

02 The Solution

The broker, agentified

agent-resolv is the broker agent. It sits between the consumer — human or AI agent — and the provider (insurer or bank). It ingests the ask, compiles information, routes to providers, analyses responses, and returns a recommendation. It is not a chatbot. It is not a comparison site. It is the broker — a complete agentification of brokerage as software.

The entity holds dual ASF corretor de seguros and BdP credit intermediary licenses, enabling fully independent, client-first advice across both insurance and lending.

Customer-In, Product-Out

agent-resolv inverts the industry entirely. We start with the customer:

  1. What do you want to protect?
  2. How much coverage?
  3. For what specifically?
  4. At what price point?

Only then do we find products that match — across all providers, independently, without bias. The customer defines the requirement; we execute the search. This is not a UX improvement. It is a fundamentally different business model. Because we are licensed and independent, that trust is contractual — not marketing.

"A Gente Resolve"

Portuguese for "we sort it out." Also: agent + resolv. Triple meaning: the AI agent, the technical resolve, and the human promise. The customer doesn't need to become an expert — they tell us what they need, we take it from there. Trust is the product. Everything else is execution.

Service as Software

agent-resolv is not a product customers subscribe to — it is a service that executes end-to-end. The broker agent does what a broker does: information gathering, multi-provider quoting, comparison, recommendation, application processing, policy management. The human layer — licensing, regulatory accountability, judgment on complex cases — is retained through qualified human directors. The result: AI speed and scale with human accountability.

03 Why Now

Five converging factors. An 18–36 month window.

These five factors compound — they do not trade off. Miss the window, and the next entry point is post-FiDA (2027–28), when APIs commoditise access and incumbents have rebuilt defensibly.

  • 1. IDD Consolidation
    The Insurance Distribution Directive's implementation triggered compliance-driven consolidation. Intermediaries fell 37% (16,783 → 10,489) from 2019–2023. Weak independent agents are exiting. The structural gap between SME demand for advice and intermediary supply is widening rapidly. That gap is a market.
  • 2. FiDA Standard-Setting (2027–28)
    The Financial Data Access regulation will mandate standardised, API-based access to insurance data. Early movers who already have large customer bases and provider integrations will own the transition. Entrants after APIs are standardised compete on brand and execution only. The window to build customer lock-in before standardisation narrows to 18–24 months.
  • 3. The Mortgage Boom
    Mortgage lending hit €23.3B in 2025 — the highest since 2014. Every mortgage triggers simultaneous demand for insurance: life, property, mortgage protection. No incumbent combines mortgage and insurance advisory in a single conversation. The cross-sell opportunity is immediate and quantifiable.
  • 4. Digital Channel Infrastructure is Mature
    WhatsApp Cloud API, voice AI (ElevenLabs, Vapi), email automation, and A2A protocols are all production-ready. Omni-channel intake is buildable today at a fraction of the cost three years ago. The infrastructure risk is eliminated.
  • 5. AI Capability Threshold Crossed
    Large language models now handle 80–90% of broker intermediation workflow: intake structuring, provider matching, comparison synthesis, edge case routing. Human oversight remains mandatory for regulatory accountability. But the AI can execute 80%+ of routine work autonomously. The "why now for AI brokers?" question is answered by capability, not wishful thinking.

05 Product

One broker agent. Any channel. Persistent memory.

5.1 Omni-Channel Architecture

The channel is not a distribution decision — it is a product principle. The customer chooses how they come in; agent-resolv meets them there. Every channel normalises to the same structured intake request before touching the broker agent. The broker agent never knows which channel it came from.

Different customer segments have permanent channel preferences — these are not transitional. Designing for one channel and adding others later means rebuilding the intake layer. agent-resolv is omni-channel from day one.

5.2 Customer Profiling

Before the broker agent routes to any provider, it builds a structured customer profile through conversational intake: business type, sector, employee count, turnover, existing coverage, claims history, budget. Profiling is channel-adaptive — spoken on a voice call, message-by-message on WhatsApp, async on email. It is never a form. Partial profiles are saved and intake resumes exactly where it left off on the next contact. Returning customers are never re-profiled from scratch.

5.3 Persistent Customer Account — The Compounding Asset

Every customer gets a persistent account on first contact — regardless of which channel they arrived through. The account holds:

  • Structured customer profile — business type, sector, headcount, turnover, risk appetite, budget
  • Current insurance policies and credit facilities — full financial picture, always up to date
  • Ongoing processes and open requests — quotes in progress, applications pending
  • Preference settings — coverage priorities, preferred providers, communication channel
  • Opportunities — coverage gaps, renewal optimisations, cross-sell moments

This is the compounding asset: the broker agent gets better per customer over time. Each interaction enriches the profile; each renewal is a retention moment; each cross-sell multiplies LTV. No comparison site has this. No traditional broker has it structured and accessible to an AI system. This is a durable moat.

5.4 Machine-Readable Endpoint (Agent API)

agent-resolv publishes a structured, machine-readable service description — a toggle on the landing page and a dedicated endpoint. Tells AI agents exactly: what we do, what we accept, what we return, which channels are live, what our licensing status is. AI agents are first-class clients. A2A-ready architecture from day one.

08 Regulatory & Licensing

Compliance is the product, not a constraint

No precedent exists in any EU jurisdiction for AI as a licensed entity. The only viable compliance structure is a licensed entity with AI tools — a properly licensed brokerage with qualified human directors, deploying AI as operational tooling under human oversight. This is how Harper, Meshed, Flow Specialty, and every other AI-native broker globally is structured.

8.2 The Strategic Choice: Corretor, not Agente

Portuguese law draws a clean structural distinction. An agente de seguros acts on behalf of insurers — their primary relationship is with the insurer, not the client. A corretor de seguros exercises distribution activity independently — their privileged contractual relationship is with the client, with a legal duty to act in the client's best interest (Art. 35 RJDS). The categories are mutually exclusive. There are only 68 corretoras in Portugal across a €14.3B market.

"A gente resolve" as a brand promise — and the customer-in model — is only legally grounded with the corretor license. The corretor license also passports EU-wide under IDD; an agente license does not.

Critical Constraint

The responsável técnico is the binding constraint — a board member with 5 consecutive or interpolated years of qualifying insurance experience within the 7-year period preceding the application. The pool of available, willing, and unconflicted candidates is inherently small. Rolando (Gonçalo's father-in-law, 20+ year licensed agent) is the primary candidate. A second candidate is needed to eliminate single-point-of-failure risk.

8.6 Credit Intermediation — Dual-Entity Structure

A corretor de seguros cannot simultaneously be an intermediário de crédito não vinculado (independent). The corporate object requirements conflict. If agent-resolv wants genuine independence in both insurance and credit, it requires a two-entity corporate structure — one for the corretor license (ASF), one for the non-tied credit intermediary license (BdP). This requires legal counsel confirmation before entity formation.

8.9 Agent-First Path — Viable Fallback

If the responsável técnico cannot be secured within 2–3 months: launch as an agente with a clear roadmap to upgrade within 18–24 months. Eliminates the €50,000 capital requirement, guarantee bond, and 5-year experience requirement. Gets to market 6–12 months faster. The costs: no legal independence, reduced duty of care. MUDEY took exactly this path.

Appendix A

Platform Vision

This section represents longer-term strategic thinking, not locked decisions.

The underlying thesis: any market where suppliers structure information to their advantage and consumers navigate complexity alone is attackable with the same model. The beachhead is insurance + credit. But the arc is agent-resolv as a platform: /irs-declaration → /energy → /phone-internet → and beyond.

Agent economy track: A separate track for agent-to-agent services — a knowledge marketplace where AI agents pay for structured analysis. Architecturally distinct from consumer intermediation. One AI agent calls another to quote a specific risk scenario; agent-resolv is the counterparty providing licensed brokerage at machine speed and pricing. This track is not modelled in Y1–Y3 financials but is the long-term moat against aggregation.

EU expansion (Year 3+): Corretor license passports to Spain, France, Italy. Duplicate entity structure in each market, leveraging shared AI infrastructure. Year 3 target: 2–3 EU markets, 10,000+ combined active clients.

Data and analytics layer (Year 4+): Portfolio risk analytics, market trend intelligence, premium optimisation. Monetise the compounded customer data as a service layer to insurers, reinsurers, and brokers.
