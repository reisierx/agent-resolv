Executive Summary

The thesis in four paragraphs

The Opportunity

Portugal's insurance market reached €14.3B in gross written premiums in 2024. Fewer than 1% of premiums are sold online. The country has just 68 licensed independent brokers serving 1.1 million SMEs — 74% of which are underinsured. Credit intermediation exploded from 9% to 57% of all mortgage volume in five years, proving Portuguese consumers adopt intermediary-led financial services rapidly when they perceive value. No AI-native insurance or credit intermediary exists. The regulatory environment is tightening — 93.9% of credit intermediaries found irregular by Banco de Portugal.

The Solution

agent-resolv is an AI-native broker agent for credit and insurance intermediation. It sits between the consumer — human or AI agent — and the provider. It ingests requests via any channel (WhatsApp, Telegram, email, voice, or API), compiles information, routes to providers, analyses responses, and returns recommendations. It is not a chatbot or comparison site. It is the broker — a complete agentification of brokerage as software, holding dual ASF corretor de seguros and BdP independent credit intermediary licenses.

Go-to-Market

Phase 1 targets SMEs with mandatory insurance (workers' compensation, professional liability) bundled with voluntary coverage. Intake is omni-channel — email, voice, and WhatsApp depending on the segment. Credit intermediation — specifically mortgage referrals — serves as the customer acquisition wedge. Phase 2 scales through B2B2C embedded distribution: Revolut (2.1M Portuguese users), Rauva, Coverflex (250K+ users), and Idealista (38M monthly visitors). Phase 3 positions agent-resolv for the emerging agent economy via A2A and AP2 protocols.

The Ask

A pre-seed round of €250–500K to fund licensing (~€50K year-1), a lean founding team, provider integration, and a stealth pilot proving the model with live SME customers. BuildUp Labs' involvement provides structured acceleration, Lisbon-based fintech network access, and credibility with Portuguese regulators and providers that capital alone cannot deliver. If BuL incubates, BuL serves as technical co-founder — not an advisor — with equity and full ownership of technical execution.

01 Market & Opportunity

A near-blank canvas in a €1.4B commission market

Three converging forces create the opportunity. Agentic AI permeation: deterministic human workflows are being absorbed by AI agents. Credit and insurance intermediation is 80–90% operational drudgery — AI compresses the operational layer; the human layer (licensing, accountability, trust) remains essential but scarce. On-chain asset layer: every financial asset class is being tokenised. On-chain reinsurance is already live ($130M+ pooled). Google's AP2 agent payment protocol is backed by Mastercard, Revolut, Coinbase, and PayPal. Compliance as durable moat: EU insurance distribution requires licensed natural persons. The EU AI Act classifies credit scoring and life/health insurance as high-risk AI. For AI-native operators who build compliance from day one, these requirements are architectural decisions. For traditional brokers, they are prohibitively expensive retrofits.

Customer Segments

Primary — SMEs (1.1M enterprises, 95% micro)

Portugal's SME sector is enormous, underinsured, and digitally underserved. 74% of Portuguese SMEs carry inadequate coverage. 88% cannot explain what their professional liability insurance covers — the highest rate in Europe. One in three SME owners has not reviewed their policies in over three years. The traditional intermediary network serving these businesses is contracting rapidly: registered intermediaries fell 37% from 16,783 (2019) to 10,489 (2023). Surviving individual agents average just €11,717 per year in commissions — below the national minimum wage. The gap between SME demand and intermediary supply is widening.

Secondary — Personal lines

Portugal's insurance density is €1,350 per capita (5.2% of GDP), below the EU average of ~7.5%. Online insurance sales account for under 1% of premiums. Customer satisfaction is poor — no Portuguese insurer has achieved a "high satisfaction" rating in DECO PROteste surveys. ASF received 28,939 formal complaints in 2024.

Tertiary — Expat segment (1.54M residents, growing 18%/yr)

Portugal's foreign resident population has nearly quadrupled since 2017. Concentrated in four coastal districts. Acute, underserved insurance and credit needs: visa-stage health, property, motor, life for mortgages, and credit itself. Only a handful of specialized brokers offer English-language services. No provider offers a unified expat insurance journey.

ICP — Phase 1

Portuguese micro-enterprise (1–9 employees) in a regulated profession — accountants, architects, engineers, lawyers, medical practitioners, real estate agents. Mandatory insurance creates a non-negotiable purchase trigger. Cluster: Lisbon and Porto (57% of all SMEs). Owner demographic: 35–54 (peak WhatsApp engagement). Currently served by an aging, under-resourced agent network earning below minimum wage.

Competition

Direct competitors in Portugal: effectively none. No AI-native insurance or credit broker exists. The closest analogue is MUDEY, Portugal's first digital insurance mediator — but MUDEY holds only an agent license (not a broker), has raised just €720K, employs 10–15 people, and has ~30,000 registered users. MUDEY's pivot to B2B tooling for micro-mediators signals difficulty in D2C acquisition.

Why Now

Five converging factors: (1) IDD consolidation is eliminating weak intermediaries — 37% headcount reduction since 2019, predicted to halve again — creating a vacuum in SME advisory; (2) FiDA regulation (2027–28) will force standardized API access to insurance data, rewarding early movers with established client relationships; (3) the mortgage boom (€23.3B in 2025 new lending, highest since 2014) generates cross-sell demand for insurance; (4) WhatsApp infrastructure is mature — 90% penetration, 98% open rates, Cloud API stable; (5) AI capability has crossed the threshold where an autonomous broker agent can handle 80–90% of the intermediation workflow.

02 Solution & Product

The broker, agentified

agent-resolv does what a broker does — end-to-end. Information gathering, multi-provider quoting, comparison, recommendation, application processing, policy management. The human layer — licensing, regulatory accountability, judgment on complex cases — is retained through qualified directors. The result is a service, not a product: agent-resolv does what a broker does, but at AI speed and scale.

Core Design Philosophy

Customer-in, product-out. The industry works backwards: suppliers design pre-packaged products → brokers present what earns the most commission → customers receive 80-page PDFs and decipher them alone. agent-resolv inverts this entirely. We start with the customer — what do you need to protect, how much coverage, at what price — and then find what fits, across all providers, without bias. Because we are licensed and independent, that trust is contractual.

Omni-Channel Architecture

The channel is not a distribution decision — it is a product principle. The customer chooses how they come in; agent-resolv meets them there. Every channel (WhatsApp, Telegram, email, voice, web, API/A2A) normalises to the same structured intake request before touching the broker agent. The broker agent never knows which channel it came from. One broker, any channel, consistent experience. Different segments have permanent channel preferences — designing for one channel and adding others later means rebuilding the intake layer. agent-resolv is omni-channel from day one.

Three User Journeys

Journey 1 — SME workers' comp + liability

A Porto-based architectural firm (6 employees) contacts agent-resolv — via email, phone, or WhatsApp depending on their preference. The broker agent conducts structured intake: sector, employee count, annual turnover, existing coverage, claims history. It identifies mandatory workers' compensation, mandatory professional liability (architects: regulated profession), and recommends voluntary cyber insurance. Routes to Fidelidade, Allianz, and Zurich in parallel. Within 48 hours: a comparison table — three options, each explained in plain language. Owner selects via their preferred channel. Policy binding initiated. Total elapsed time: 3–5 days vs. 2–4 weeks through a traditional broker.

Journey 2 — Mortgage + life + home insurance

A young couple buying their first home in Lisbon, embedded in their Idealista property search. Broker agent pre-populates listing data, initiates structured intake: income, employment, deposit, target value. Routes simultaneously to five banks for mortgage and — anticipating bank requirements — bundles life insurance and mandatory fire coverage. The couple receives a unified view: mortgage terms from five banks alongside matched life and home insurance quotes. One conversation, three products.

Journey 3 — Agent-to-agent via API

A Revolut user's AI financial assistant identifies an insurance need. It routes to agent-resolv via A2A protocol, receives quotes in structured machine-readable format, and presents recommendations — no human intermediary in the loop except for compliance oversight. agent-resolv publishes a machine-readable service description: intake schema, output format, channel availability, licensing status. AI agents are first-class clients from day one.

03 Regulatory & Licensing

Compliance is the product, not a constraint

No precedent exists in any EU jurisdiction for AI as a licensed entity. IDD requirements are anthropocentric: they require natural persons with professional qualifications. The only viable compliance structure is a licensed entity with AI tools — a properly licensed brokerage with qualified human directors, deploying AI as operational tooling under human oversight. This is how Harper, Meshed, Flow Specialty, and every other AI-native broker globally is structured.

LicenseRequirementsTimelineYear-1 Cost
ASF Corretor de Seguros PII ≥€1.3M, 120h certified training, qualified director (5yr experience), clean criminal record 3–6 months ~€50,000
BdP Credit Intermediary Professional certification, liability insurance. Digital-only operation explicitly permitted. 2–4 months ~€10,000
Qualified Director Solution

Rolando — Gonçalo's father-in-law, licensed insurance agent with 20+ years of professional experience — satisfies the 5-year experienced person requirement and serves as qualified director during the licensing phase. This is not a workaround. It is the standard path for new brokerage entrants in Portugal.

EU AI Act (August 2026 deadline): agent-resolv's credit and insurance AI is classified high-risk (Annex III, Section 5). Compliance requirements — full technical documentation, human oversight for binding recommendations, bias testing, conformity assessment — are architectural decisions for a day-one AI-native operator. For incumbents, they are expensive retrofits. The compliance moat compounds over time as regulatory scrutiny increases.

The moat operates on three levels: (1) licensing scarcity — only 68 corretores in Portugal; (2) escalating regulatory enforcement — ASF conducted 249 suspensions and 783 cancellations in 2024; (3) EU AI Act creates a second barrier for any incumbent attempting to add AI after August 2026.
