## What's Spot-On

**The B2B2C recommendation is correct** for our stage and market. The LTV:CAC math alone makes it the obvious starting point. Rolando as both qualified director and pilot mediador is the single biggest unfair advantage we have — the competitive intelligence doc makes this explicit in a way that will land well with investors.

**"Schema is the IP" is the right framing** for our defensible asset. Not the AI, not the interface — the normalized representation of Portuguese insurance and credit products. This needs to be front and centre in the pitch.

**The email integration reframe is correct.** Stop treating it as a limitation, start positioning it as "we automated the mediation layer that even Sequoia-backed US players still do manually."

---

## One Push-Back

The B2B2C model has a structural risk the doc underweights: **mediador dependency**. The risk isn't just that mediadores leave with clients — it's that we become a productivity tool for them without ever owning the customer relationship.

The evolution from B2B2C to direct B2B needs to be deliberate and sequenced, not assumed. We need a concrete trigger for when we start the direct acquisition motion — probably when we hit X mediadores on the platform and have proof of brand recognition from the SME side. If we don't define this proactively, we risk getting trapped as a SaaS tool for mediadores indefinitely.

---

## One Gap

The credit side is mentioned but underdeveloped throughout. The dual ASF + BdP license is called a moat — correctly — but there's no analysis of how credit brokerage integrates into the mediador model:
- Does Rolando's existing network have credit intermediation needs?
- When does credit come up in the SME journey — same moment as insurance, or a separate trigger?
- Does the mediador model extend naturally to credit, or does credit require a different distribution approach?

This should be mapped before MVP design.

## Strategic Implication for the Pitch

The Sequoia piece names insurance brokerage as the largest dollar market in their autopilot priority map ($140–200B) and calls it "pure intelligence work." WithCoverage ($42M) and Harper ($46.8M) are the named plays. agent-resolv is the Portuguese version — with the additional moat of a dual license and a WhatsApp-native interface for a market where dashboard adoption is low.

The investor narrative writes itself: global wave validated by top-tier capital, Portuguese market at <1% online penetration, first-mover in dual-licensed AI-native intermediation.
