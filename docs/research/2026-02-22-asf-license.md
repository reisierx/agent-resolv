# ASF Licensing Research: Insurance & Credit Intermediation in Portugal
**Research date:** 2026-02-22  
**Commissioned for:** agent-resolv.com thesis evaluation  
**Compiled by:** Fred (ReisierX)

---

## Executive Summary

- **Licensing is accessible, not a moat.** Both ASF (insurance) and Banco de Portugal (credit) intermediary licenses are achievable by motivated individuals. The credit intermediary process saw 8,902 applications up to end-2023, with 5,822 active registrations — proof of mass-market accessibility, not elite gatekeeping.
- **Two separate regulatory regimes.** Insurance mediators are supervised by ASF; credit intermediaries are supervised by Banco de Portugal. A single operator wanting both must navigate two separate authorization processes, but there is no structural bar to holding both.
- **Costs are modest for agents/tied intermediaries; higher for independent brokers.** An insurance agent needs only €5,000 share capital. An insurance broker (independent, full) needs €50,000 + PI insurance + organized accounting + 3-year business plan. A tied credit intermediary can use the lender's PI insurance — near-zero direct cost.
- **Timeline is 2–6 months per license.** Statutory review deadlines are 60–90 days for insurance, with practical timelines (including preparation + review + registration) of 3–6 months. An existing licensed broker adding a second license type is faster.
- **⭐ The single most important insight for agent-resolv.com:** Licensing is a moderate friction barrier, not a hard moat. The real differentiation is not the license itself but the **trust networks, local relationships, and compliance track record** of established brokers. The supply-side expansion risk is moderate (3–6 months to onboard each new broker) — manageable but not trivial. Existing dual-licensed brokers (insurance + credit) are the highest-value nodes in the network.

## 1. License Types

### 1A. Insurance Intermediaries (ASF-supervised)

**Legal framework:** Lei n.º 7/2019, de 16 de janeiro (Insurance Distribution Law), transposing the EU Insurance Distribution Directive (IDD 2016/97/EU). Supervised by **ASF** (Autoridade de Supervisão de Seguros e Fundos de Pensões).

Three categories exist:

| Category | Acts for | Activity basis | Capital min | Registration filed by |
|----------|----------|----------------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Ancillary insurance intermediary** (mediador de seguros a título acessório) | Insurers | Ancillary to main business (e.g., car dealers, travel agents) | Not specified | Insurer |
| **Insurance agent** (agente de seguros) | Insurers | Main professional activity; tied to one or more insurers | €5,000 | Insurer |
| **Insurance broker** (corretor de seguros) | Clients (independent) | Exclusive; independent of insurers | €50,000 | Broker itself |

**Key restrictions by category:**
- **Ancillary:** Cannot distribute life insurance or liability risks (unless those complement their main product). Cannot distribute IBIPs (investment products). Lower qualification bar.
- **Agent:** Tied to insurer(s) via distribution contract. Cannot represent clients.
- **Broker:** Must act in clients' best interests. Exclusive corporate purpose = financial services. Subject to portfolio diversification rules (must recommend from a sufficiently large market sample).

**Banks:** Cannot register as ancillary intermediaries under the IDD. Typically registered as agents (bancassurance).

---

### 1B. Credit Intermediaries (Banco de Portugal-supervised)

**Legal framework:** Decreto-Lei n.º 81-C/2017, de 7 de julho (transposing EU Mortgage Credit Directive 2014/17/EU and Consumer Credit Directive 2008/48/EC). Supervised by **Banco de Portugal**.

Three categories:

| Category | Description | PI insurance |
|----------|-------------|--------------|
| **Tied** (intermediário de crédito vinculado) | Works on behalf of one or more specific lenders; activities under full and unconditional responsibility of the lender | Can be covered by lender's PI policy; tied intermediary does not need own policy |
| **Non-tied / Independent** (intermediário de crédito não vinculado) | Works independently; not tied to any specific lender; can advise across the market | Must hold own PI insurance |
| **Ancillary** (intermediário de crédito a título acessório) | Credit distribution is secondary to main business (e.g., car dealers, retailers offering financing) | Can be covered by lender's PI policy; lighter requirements overall |

**Scope of credit products covered:**
- Mortgage/housing credit (crédito à habitação)
- Consumer credit (crédito aos consumidores)
- Advisory services (serviços de consultoria) on credit contracts

## 2. Requirements

### 2A. Insurance Intermediaries

**Ancillary intermediary:**
- Professional qualification — lower threshold (no specific CPD hours for primary activity, but general IDD training required)
- PI insurance required but at reduced levels
- Cannot be banks
- Registration application filed by the insurer on behalf of the ancillary

**Insurance agent:**
- **Professional qualifications:** Must demonstrate competence (IDD-mandated areas: financial regulation, insurance products, claims, complaints handling, etc.)
- **CPD:** Minimum **15 hours per year** (EU IDD minimum, maintained under Lei 7/2019; Portugal "gold-plated" the IDD by imposing stricter qualification requirements overall)
- **Minimum share capital:** €5,000
- **PI insurance:** Required (IDD minimum: €1,300,000 per claim / €1,920,000 aggregate per year — from EU Delegated Regulation 2017/2358)
- **Physical establishment:** Must have at least one establishment open to the public
- **Fit and proper:** Clean criminal record; no financial disqualifications; no conflicts of interest
- **Filing:** Application submitted by the insurer to ASF

**Insurance broker:**
- **Professional qualifications:** Same IDD-mandated areas + "risk analyst" required for non-life branches
- **CPD:** Minimum 15 hours per year
- **Minimum share capital:** **€50,000** (fully paid-up at registration)
- **Exclusive corporate purpose:** Company's sole purpose must be financial services / insurance distribution
- **PI insurance:** Required at IDD minimums (€1,300,000 per claim / €1,920,000 aggregate)
- **3-year activity programme** submitted to ASF
- **Organized accounting:** Must maintain proper books
- **Physical establishment:** At least one open to public
- **Portfolio diversification:** Must survey a sufficiently broad range of products; cannot be captured by one insurer
- **Fit and proper:** Applies to shareholders, management, and key functions
- **Filing:** Broker applies directly to ASF

### 2B. Credit Intermediaries

**All categories:**
- **Professional certification** (certidão de conhecimentos e competências): Awarded by Banco de Portugal–accredited training providers after passing required training modules:
  - "Intermediação de crédito" (mandatory for all)
  - "Comercialização de crédito hipotecário" (if doing mortgage credit)
  - "Comercialização de crédito aos consumidores" (if doing consumer credit)
- **Idoneidade (fitness):** Professional conduct, no criminal convictions for relevant offences, no prior disqualifications
- **Compatibilidade:** No incompatible roles (e.g., direct employment at a competing lender, in some cases)
- **IT/admin infrastructure:** Means for electronic communication, internet access, own archive, establishment open to public
- **Disclosure of ties:** If tied, must disclose which lenders. Must post a sign stating ties.

**Tied intermediary (vinculado):**
- Lighter requirements overall
- PI insurance: Covered by the lender's policy (lender takes tomador do seguro position) — **zero direct PI cost to the intermediary**
- Lighter competency requirements than independent

**Independent intermediary (não vinculado):**
- PI insurance: Must hold own PI policy meeting conditions of **Portaria n.º 385-E/2017** (specific minimums set therein — exact figures require accessing that portaria, but structure mirrors IDD minimums)
- Can only receive remuneration from clients (not from lenders)
- Full competency requirements

**Ancillary (acessório):**
- PI covered by lender (as for tied)
- Reduced competency requirements
- E.g., a real estate agent or car dealer offering credit products in connection with their core business

## 3. Timeline

### Insurance intermediaries
| Phase | Duration |
|-------|----------|
| Application preparation (documents, qualifications, PI insurance quote) | 4–8 weeks |
| ASF review — ancillary/agent | **Statutory 60 days** from complete application |
| ASF review — broker | **Statutory 90 days** from complete application |
| Post-authorization formalities | 2–4 weeks |
| **Realistic total — agent** | **3–4 months** |
| **Realistic total — broker** | **4–6 months** |

_Note: ASF deadline clock starts only when the application is considered complete. Incomplete applications pause the clock; requests for supplementary information are common and add time in practice._

### Credit intermediaries
| Phase | Duration |
|-------|----------|
| Qualification training + exam | 4–8 weeks (courses accredited by ASF/iBCE) |
| Application preparation | 2–4 weeks |
| Banco de Portugal review | Not specified by statute; in practice **1–3 months** |
| Registration (post-authorization) | **30 days** after authorization notification |
| **Realistic total** | **3–5 months** |

**Data point:** As of December 31, 2023, Banco de Portugal had received 8,902 authorization requests total, with 82 still under active analysis on that day — implying the bulk are processed relatively efficiently.

### Insurance intermediary

| Cost item | Ancillary | Agent | Broker |
|-----------|-----------|-------|--------|
| Share capital | Not specified | €5,000 | €50,000 |

| ASF registration fee | Small (few hundred €) | Small | Small |
| ASF annual supervision fee | % of premiums intermediated | % of premiums | % of premiums |
| Legal/accounting setup | €1,000–€3,000 | €1,000–€3,000 | €3,000–€8,000 |
| Training (initial) | Minimal | €200–€500 | €500–€1,500 |
| **Indicative total first year** | **€2,000–€5,000** | **€7,000–€12,000** | **€60,000–€80,000** |

_PI insurance premiums are estimates based on market comparables; exact pricing depends on volume, claims history, and insurer._

### Credit intermediary

| Cost item | Tied | Independent | Ancillary |
|-----------|------|-------------|-----------|
| Share capital | None mandated | None mandated | None mandated |
| PI insurance | **Zero** (covered by lender) | Portaria 385-E/2017 minimum (estimated €500–€2,000/yr) | Zero (covered by lender) |
| Banco de Portugal registration fee | ~€100–€500 | ~€100–€500 | ~€100–€500 |
| Training/certification | €200–€600 per person | €200–€600 per person | €200–€600 per person |
| **Indicative total first year (tied)** | **€500–€2,000** | **€1,500–€4,000** | **€500–€1,500** |

_Key observation: Tied credit intermediaries face almost no direct financial barrier. This is by design — the DL 81-C/2017 preamble explicitly acknowledges lighter requirements for tied and ancillary categories given the lender's unconditional responsibility._

## 5. Renewal & Ongoing Compliance Burden

### Insurance intermediaries (ASF)
- **Annual CPD:** Minimum **15 hours per year** of professional training (IDD-mandated; Portugal may impose additional requirements via ASF norms)
- **Annual reporting:** Financial statements, activity data, complaints statistics reported to ASF
- **Complaints book (Livro de Reclamações):** Mandatory; physical and online complaints register
- **Portfolio diversification monitoring (brokers):** Ongoing obligation to demonstrate independence and breadth
- **Customer information obligations:** Pre-sale suitability analysis, best-interest documentation for each client
- **Changes notification:** Any change in management, capital, or activity scope requires ASF notification
- **ASF supervision fee:** Calculated as a percentage of premiums mediated annually (small but recurring)
- **Market conduct supervision:** ASF actively monitors complaints and can initiate on-site inspections

**Assessment:** Light to moderate. A small one-person operation (mediador singular) has relatively low overhead. The 15h CPD per year is about 2 days of training, widely available through APROSE, APS-accredited providers, and online platforms.

### Credit intermediaries (Banco de Portugal)
- **Annual CPD:** Required (specific hours not confirmed at time of research; ongoing training culture established)
- **Annual reporting:** Activity and financial information to Banco de Portugal
- **Disclosure obligations:** Must always disclose ties, remuneration model, and whether they can advise independently
- **Client information duties:** Pre-contract ESIS (European Standardised Information Sheet) for mortgages; standardized disclosure for consumer credit
- **Complaint handling:** Must have documented complaint process and report to regulator
- **Changes notification:** Banco de Portugal must be notified of material changes

**Assessment:** Light to moderate. As a tied intermediary (the natural model for Rolando-type brokers working with specific banks), the lender handles most compliance infrastructure. Independent intermediaries carry more burden.

## 6. Accessibility Assessment

### Is this a realistic barrier or a moat?

**Short answer: Moderate friction barrier, not a moat.**

**Evidence for accessibility:**
1. **Volume of registrations:** 8,902 credit intermediary applications by end-2023; 5,822 active registrations. This is not an elite club. Individual real estate agents, car dealers, financial advisors — all going through this process.
2. **Insurance agent entry:** €5,000 capital, filed by the insurer (the insurer does the paperwork), 60-day statutory review. A motivated individual working with one insurer can be registered in 2–3 months with minimal cost.
3. **Tied credit intermediary:** Near-zero capital and PI cost if the lender covers it. Training is the main barrier — a few weeks of coursework.
4. **The training is structured and available:** Multiple accredited providers (AEP, APROSE, APS, Distance Learning Consulting, MOFP, SABFORMA, Zurich) offer recognized courses. Not arcane.

**Evidence for real friction:**
1. **Insurance brokers** (independent, full) do face meaningful barriers: €50,000 capital, exclusive corporate purpose, 3-year business plan, PI insurance, organized accounting. This limits casual entry at the broker tier.
2. **Timeline is 3–6 months minimum.** You cannot onboard a new broker overnight. Supply-side expansion has inherent latency.
3. **The fit-and-proper regime creates real screening.** Financial history, criminal record, professional competence are all scrutinized. Bad actors are filtered out, adding legitimacy to the licensed intermediary pool.
4. **Dual licensing (insurance + credit) requires separate applications to separate regulators.** Not prohibitive but adds process.

### Could Rolando (licensed broker in Sardoal) easily add new intermediation types?

**Highly likely yes, with important nuances:**
- If Rolando holds an ASF insurance mediator registration, adding a credit intermediary authorization from Banco de Portugal is a **separate process but not a high barrier** — primarily requires completing the credit training modules and a Banco de Portugal application.
- The reverse is also true: a credit intermediary adding insurance distribution requires ASF registration (typically as an agent, filed by an insurer they partner with).
- Expanding insurance categories (e.g., from non-life to life insurance) may require additional ASF notification/authorization and additional training.
- The natural path for an existing local broker like Rolando: **already has ASF insurance registration → add credit intermediary registration (tied, with one or more banks) → relatively quick, low-cost, lender handles PI insurance.**

### Could a new entrant get licensed within 6 months?

**Yes, for most categories, with focused effort:**
- Insurance agent: 3–4 months realistic
- Tied credit intermediary: 3–5 months realistic
- Insurance broker (independent, full): **borderline** — 4–6 months possible but capital setup, company formation, and 3-year plan add complexity. More likely 6–9 months for a fully compliant new entity.

## 7. Implications for agent-resolv.com

### The supply-side dynamics

**The licensing regime creates structure, not scarcity.** There are ~5,800 registered credit intermediaries in Portugal. The insurance intermediary population is larger (including thousands of agents and brokers tied to insurers). This is a distributed, relatively accessible population — good news for a routing/marketplace model.

**Key structural insight:** The licensing regime creates **tiered supply** with meaningful differentiation:
- **Tier 1 — Independent brokers** (corretores de seguros, intermediários de crédito não vinculados): Highest optionality, can represent any lender/insurer, act in client's interest. Relatively scarce (full broker license is more demanding). High-value nodes for agent-resolv.
- **Tier 2 — Tied agents** (agentes de seguros, intermediários vinculados): More common. Can only present products from their tied insurer(s)/lender(s). Useful for coverage breadth but limited flexibility.
- **Tier 3 — Ancillary** (mediadores/intermediários acessórios): Incidental to another business. Lowest compliance burden, lowest optionality.

**For agent-resolv.com:** The routing value is highest when working with **Tier 1 independent brokers** — they can match any product to any client without tied restrictions. But the supply of Tier 1 is more limited and more expensive to maintain (higher compliance cost). Tier 2 agents are the workhorse of the market but structurally constrained.

### Is it a moat or a commodity?

**Neither extreme. It's a structured playing field.**

The ASF/BdP licensing regime means:
- **Barrier to entry exists** — casual or fraudulent operators are filtered out. The license itself has value as a trust signal.
- **But the barrier is crossable** — in 3–6 months, any reasonably capitalized, clean-record professional can get licensed.
- **The real moat is not the license** — it is the combination of:
  1. **Established client relationships** (multi-year, local trust)
  2. **Compliance track record** (no sanctions, clean history with regulators)
  3. **Product expertise** (knowing which mortgages/policies actually work for which situations)
  4. **Geographic coverage** (covering Sardoal — not many brokers want to serve sub-10,000 population municipalities)

### Specific implications

1. **Onboarding new brokers takes 3–6 months minimum.** agent-resolv.com should not assume it can rapidly expand supply. Existing licensed brokers are the fastest path to network scale.

2. **Rolando-type brokers in small municipalities are the strategic sweet spot.** They already hold licenses, have local trust, and face almost no digital competition. They're also systematically underserved by fintech because their geographies are uneconomical for direct online models.

3. **The tied vs. independent structure matters for the revenue model.** A tied credit intermediary cannot charge the client — only the lender. An independent one can charge the client. If agent-resolv routes a mortgage via a tied intermediary, the intermediary earns a referral fee from the bank. If routed via an independent, the model is different. The platform's revenue sharing needs to account for these structural differences.

4. **Dual licensing (insurance + credit) is achievable but rare.** A broker who holds both is twice as valuable as a routing node. agent-resolv should consider whether its broker onboarding flow includes support for adding the second license type.

5. **Compliance as infrastructure, not overhead.** The 15h CPD per year, annual reporting, etc., are lightweight enough that they don't create ongoing friction to staying in the network. A broker who is already licensed stays licensed without significant incremental effort.

6. **ASF supervision (for insurance) is more active than BdP supervision (for credit).** Insurance brokers face more ongoing scrutiny. This is relevant for platform liability design — if a broker has a poor ASF track record (complaints, sanctions), that's a quality signal the platform should incorporate.

## ⭐ Single Most Important Insight for agent-resolv.com

**The licensing regime does not protect incumbents — it structures the playing field.**

The 5,800+ registered credit intermediaries and thousands of insurance agents/brokers mean the **supply side is not scarce by regulation**. What makes individual brokers like Rolando in Sardoal valuable is not their license — it's the local trust, established client base, and geographic commitment to markets no one else serves.

agent-resolv.com's moat, if it has one, is **aggregating and activating this distributed, licensed-but-undermonetized network** faster than any competitor. The licensing creates a natural quality floor (screened entrants, ongoing compliance obligations), but it does not create scarcity. The platform must create its own network effects — stickiness of data, cross-referral loops between brokers, and client relationship ownership — because the license alone will not lock anyone in.

Put differently: **licensing is necessary but not sufficient for competitive defensibility**. The question is not "can someone get licensed?" (yes, anyone can in 3–6 months) but "why would a newly licensed broker join agent-resolv instead of going direct or joining a competitor network?"
