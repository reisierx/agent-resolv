# Portugal Consumer & Demand Landscape — Insurance and Credit Intermediation

> **Date:** 2026-02-25
> **Author:** Claude (research assist for ReisierX)
> **Purpose:** Profile the demand side — who the customers are, how large each segment is, how they currently buy insurance and credit products, and where the pain is. SMEs are the primary target segment.

---

## Executive Summary

Portugal's 1.1 million SMEs represent a massive, underinsured, and digitally underserved market ripe for disruption. Seven in ten Portuguese SMEs carry inadequate coverage, online insurance sales account for less than 1% of premiums, and the number of licensed intermediaries has shrunk 37% in four years — yet demand is surging. The total insurance market hit **€14.3 billion in 2024** (+21%), credit intermediaries now originate **57% of all mortgages** (up from 9% in 2019), and a foreign-resident population of **1.54 million** creates an entirely underserved parallel market. For ReisierX, the convergence of mandatory insurance demand, a fragmented broker landscape, high WhatsApp penetration (~90%), and growing digital comfort creates a textbook opportunity for a tech-enabled intermediary.

## 1. SME Segment

### 1.1 How Many SMEs?

Portugal's non-financial business economy comprises **1,104,691 SMEs** under the EU SBA definition (2024 estimates), representing 99.9% of all enterprises. When INE's broader scope is applied — including agriculture, health, education, and sole proprietors — the count rises to approximately **1.5 million**. These businesses employ **76.3%** of the private-sector workforce and generate **67.9%** of value added.

**Breakdown by size (EU definitions):**

| Category | Count | Share |
|---|---|---|
| Micro (<10 employees) | 1,056,056 | 95.5% |
| Small (10–49 employees) | 41,884 | 3.8% |
| Medium (50–249 employees) | 6,751 | 0.6% |
| Large (250+ employees) | 1,117 | 0.1% |

Regionally, the North (501,688 SMEs) and Greater Lisbon (357,079) together hold 57% of all businesses.

**Key sectors by enterprise count:**

| Sector | Enterprises | Turnover |
|---|---|---|
| Wholesale & retail trade | 217,094 | €116.3B |
| Accommodation & restaurants | 125,617 | — |
| Agriculture | 120,602 | — |
| Health & social services | 118,517 | — |
| Construction | 107,774 | — |
| Manufacturing | 68,745 | €57.2B |

Average SME value added per employee is approximately **€22,900**, roughly half the EU average.

*Sources: EU SBA Fact Sheet 2025 (EC); INE — International Day of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises press release, June 2025.*

### 1.2 Mandatory Insurance

Portuguese law requires every employer and self-employed worker to carry:

- **Workers' compensation insurance** (*seguro de acidentes de trabalho*) — governed by Lei n.º 98/2009. Generates approximately **€1.1 billion in annual premiums**. Distributed almost entirely (>95%) through mediation channels.
- **Motor vehicle insurance** — mandatory third-party civil liability for all registered vehicles. Generates **€2.1 billion in premiums** across 8.9 million insured vehicles.
- **Fire insurance** — legally required for any premises under the *propriedade horizontal* regime (most commercial urban properties). Typically bundled into multi-peril policies.
- **Professional liability insurance** — mandatory for a long list of regulated professions: lawyers, accountants, architects, engineers, medical practitioners, real estate agents, insurance mediators, travel agencies, and private security companies.
- **Sector-specific mandates** — construction sites, fuel distribution, rail/air transport, and sporting activities each have specific mandatory covers.

*Sources: MMC.pt; InvoiceXpress; Alfa Seguros; Seguitex; Lei n.º 98/2009.*

### 1.3 Voluntary Coverage (Beyond Mandatory)

- **Multi-peril business insurance** (*multirriscos empresas*) — fire, water damage, theft, business interruption, public liability, electronic equipment. ~€1.1B premiums (2023, +11% YoY).
- **Health insurance** — fastest-growing segment at **€1.56B** (+17.5% in 2024). Increasingly offered by SMEs as employee retention. **4 million people** now covered.
- **Cyber insurance** — globally only 10–15% of SMEs have purchased it; Portuguese micro-enterprise penetration estimated at 5–10%. **54% of Portuguese SMEs** suffered a cyberattack in the past year.
- **D&O, credit insurance, product liability, key person insurance, fleet cover** — penetration data for Portuguese SMEs is sparse.

**The protection gap is enormous:** The Hiscox Protection Gap Report 2025 found that **74% of Portuguese SMEs have a global protection gap**, **55% lack basic general liability or property coverage**, and **88% cannot explain what professional liability insurance covers** (highest in Europe). One in three SME owners hasn't reviewed their policies in over three years.

*Sources: APS — Seguros em Portugal 2023/2024; ASF; Hiscox Protection Gap Report 2025; Chubb SME Cyber Guide; Jornal Económico.*

### 1.4 How SMEs Currently Purchase Insurance and Credit

**Insurance distribution channels (non-life):**

| Channel | Share of Non-Life Premiums |
|---|---|
| Agents & brokers (non-bank) | ~76.6% |
| Bancassurance | ~15.8% |
| Direct (insurer) | ~7.6% |
| Online | <1% |

**Life insurance** flows predominantly through banks: bancassurance holds **72.8%** of life premiums.

**Intermediary landscape consolidation:**
- Total registered intermediaries fell from **16,783 in 2019 to 10,489 in 2023** (−37%).
- **6,660 individual agents** average just **€11,717/year** in commissions — below the national minimum wage.
- Only **69 licensed brokers** in the entire country, earning collectively **€217M** in commissions (avg. €3.15M per broker).
- Structural gap: 1M+ micro-enterprises need commercial insurance, but the broker channel is tiny and the agent channel is economically unsustainable for advisory-intensive SME products.

**Credit distribution:** See Section 3.

*Sources: APS; ASF — APROSE Congress speech; ECO/Sapo; EIOPA IDD Country Analysis.*

## 2. Personal Lines Insurance Market

### 2.1 Market Size

Total insurance production in Portugal reached **€14.3 billion in 2024** (+21% YoY), surpassing the previous high of €14B. Non-life premiums were approximately **€5.6 billion** (+8.6%), while life premiums were approximately **€8.7 billion** (+30%, driven by savings products).

**Key personal lines:**

| Line | Premiums (approx.) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Auto | €2.1B | 8.9M insured vehicles |
| Health | €1.56B | +17.5% in 2024; 4M covered |
| Home / multi-peril | ~€1.1B | +11% YoY |
| Life (risk) | ~€0.8B | Separate from savings/PPR |
| Life (savings/PPR) | ~€7.9B | Bank-driven |
| Workers' comp | ~€1.1B | Mandatory for employers |

Portugal's insurance density (premiums per capita) is approximately **€1,350**, and insurance penetration is approximately **5.2% of GDP** — below the EU average of ~7.5%.

*Sources: APS; ECO/Sapo; Dinheiro Vivo; ASF Observatory.*

### 2.2 Digital Adoption in Insurance

- **Online insurance sales remain under 1% of total premiums**, though growing ~24% annually.
- EIOPA IDD analysis: digital distribution in Portugal's life segment accounts for roughly **0.01% of GWP**.
- About **24% of Portuguese consumers** have purchased at least one insurance product online.
- Most research digitally, then complete purchase through a human intermediary.
- Fidelidade's mobile app: **1.6 million registered users** — most advanced digital presence.
- No insurer has achieved significant end-to-end digital sales.
- BCG Digital Insurance Study: Southern European markets like Portugal trail Northern peers where online rates exceed 25%.

*Sources: EIOPA Country-by-Country Analysis; BCG; ASF.*

## 3. Credit Intermediation Demand

### 3.1 Market Sizes

| Credit Segment | Outstanding (end 2024–2025) | New Origination (2024) |
|---|---|---|
| Mortgages (housing) | €101.7B → ~€111B (Oct 2025) | €17.9B (+32% YoY) |
| Consumer credit | ~€33.4B | ~€8.4B |
| Corporate loans (NFCs) | ~€74.2B | — |

Key mortgage metrics: 1.32 million active contracts; average new mortgage €142,791; avg. rate 2.84% (Sept 2025); ~10,000+ new contracts per month in 2024. Micro-enterprise lending growing fastest at +13.9% YoY (Dec 2025).

*Sources: Banco de Portugal — Relatório de Acompanhamento dos Mercados de Crédito 2024; Jornal Económico; GEE; BuyCasa.*

### 3.2 Intermediary vs. Direct Bank Share

The market has transformed since the 2018 transposition of the EU Mortgage Credit Directive (Decreto-Lei n.º 81-C/2017):

| Year | Mortgage Share via Intermediaries | Consumer Credit Share |
|---|---|---|
| 2019 | 9.2% | — |
| 2022 | ~30% | — |
| 2023 | ~45% | ~45.2% |
| 2024 | **57% of volume** (~€10.2B) | **49.9%** |

Auto credit is even more intermediary-dependent at **83.1%** of volume.

**Intermediary landscape:**
- **5,893 registered credit intermediaries** at end-2024, exceeding **6,100 by late 2025**.
- Vast majority are *vinculados* (tied to specific lender partners).
- Market leaders: DS Intermediários de Crédito (€2.44B formalized in 2024); Doutor Finanças (~€918M mortgage intermediation).
- Major banks (CGD, Santander, BCP, BPI, Novo Banco) view intermediaries as client acquisition partners.

*Sources: Idealista; Trust Investments; Doutor Finanças; SAPO; Banco de Portugal.*

### 3.3 Regulatory Scrutiny

Banco de Portugal inspected 298 credit intermediary premises in 2024 and found **irregularities in 93.9%** of them. Of 102 advertising materials reviewed, **69.6% were non-compliant**. The Bank revoked 136 authorizations and opened 419 administrative proceedings. A comprehensive revision of the credit intermediation legal framework is planned for 2025–2026.

**Implication for ReisierX:** Regulatory tightening creates opportunity for compliant, transparent operators. The 94% irregularity rate among incumbents positions a digital-first, audit-ready entrant as a differentiation play.

*Sources: ECO/Sapo; Dinheiro Vivo; CRS Advogados; Banco de Portugal.*

## 4. Digital Behaviour

### 4.1 Smartphone & Internet

- **Smartphone penetration: ~97%** of population (~8.1M users).
- Mobile internet subscribers: **10.7 million** (>100% of population due to multi-device).
- 5G users: **3.3 million** by Q3 2024.
- 89.5% of enterprises with 10+ employees provide mobile devices for professional use; micro-enterprises at 73.1% (up 24.5pp in four years).

*Sources: Statista; ANACOM; DataReportal Digital 2025 Portugal.*

### 4.2 WhatsApp Penetration

- WhatsApp penetration: **~90% of internet users** — estimated 8.3–8.4 million people.
- Most-used social platform in Portugal, ahead of Instagram (85%).
- 35–44 age demographic (heavy SME owner overlap) shows particularly strong engagement.
- WhatsApp business messages achieve **98% open rate** (vs. 20% for email).
- 26% of consumers globally prefer asynchronous messaging for financial services.

*Sources: Statista; Telecompaper; Sociallyin; Infobip.*

### 4.3 Digital Banking

- **59% of internet users** access online banking (EU avg. ~64%).
- **67% of bank account holders** use mobile banking apps.
- **MB WAY: 6.5 million users** (~75% of banked population). Handles 45% of e-commerce transactions.
- **49% of Portuguese** say they could imagine conducting all financial transactions exclusively via smartphone.

*Sources: Statista; Eurostat; Portugal Digital; Verified Market Research.*

### 4.4 Financial Services via Messaging

No Portugal-specific survey data exists on consumer willingness to manage insurance or credit through WhatsApp. However, global data is suggestive: over 60% of banks worldwide use WhatsApp for automated banking use cases. Given Portugal's near-universal WhatsApp adoption and rising mobile banking usage, a messaging-first intermediation model appears well-positioned.

EIOPA notes that "digitalization of intermediation/sales in the Portuguese market is still in early stages due to the tradition of concluding insurance business face to face." This represents both the current gap and the opportunity.

*Sources: EIOPA; AiSensy; Streebo; Infobip.*

## 5. Pain Points

### 5.1 Insurance Customer Satisfaction

- No Portuguese insurer has achieved a "high satisfaction" rating in DECO PROteste's annual consumer survey.
- Best scores: **Médis at 7.2/10** for health insurance (modest by global standards).
- Home insurance worst: no insurer exceeds 7.0/10; ok! seguros leads at 6.5.
- Marktest Reputation Index: insurance sector ranks **14th out of 25 sectors**.
- NPS scores range from Allianz at +14.4 to several major insurers in negative territory.

### 5.2 Documented Frustrations

- **Policy exclusions** are the #1 consumer complaint across all lines.
- >25% of home insurance claimants report incomplete reimbursement.
- 13.8% of health insurance customers discover coverage gaps only at point of need.
- ASF received **28,939 formal complaints in 2024** (−6.5% from 2023): auto 36%, property/fire 16%, health 11%.
- Claims disputes constitute 54–60% of all complaints.
- Resolution rate favors complainants only **39%** of the time.

### 5.3 SME-Specific Pain Points

- **74% underinsured** (Hiscox 2025).
- **55% lack basic general liability or property coverage.**
- **88% cannot explain professional liability insurance** — highest ignorance rate in Europe.
- Top SME concerns — workplace injuries (42%), theft/property damage (36%), cyberattack (38%) — map to available products, yet take-up is low.
- **54% of SMEs suffered a cyberattack** in the past year; <15% have cyber coverage.
- Fewer agents serve more enterprises, with most individual agents earning below subsistence from mediation alone.
- One in three SME owners hasn't reviewed policies in 3+ years.

### 5.4 Credit Intermediation Pain Points

- Average mortgage takes ~**45 days** from first contact to closing.
- Banco de Portugal found **93.9% irregularity rate** in on-site inspections — even as intermediaries become the dominant channel, service quality and transparency remain inconsistent.
- Portugal's extreme exposure to variable-rate mortgages (**87% of stock**, highest in EU) amplified consumer distress during 2022–2023 rate shock.
- **218,995 PERSI default proceedings** opened in three years.
- Non-compliance in advertising (69.6%) erodes trust.

*Sources: DECO PROteste; ASF; Hiscox; Executive Digest; RTP; Banco de Portugal; ECO/Sapo.*

## 6. Expat / Digital Nomad Segment

### 6.1 Size & Growth

- Foreign resident population: **1,543,697 at end-2024** (~14.4% of total population).
- Nearly quadrupled from 421,802 in 2017.
- Top nationalities: Brazilians (484,596 / 31.4%), Indians (98,616), Angolans (92,348), Ukrainians (~91,000).
- Americans: ~14,100 legal residents (doubled since 2019), lead Golden Visa applications.
- **2,600+ digital nomad visas** (D8) issued since October 2022 launch.
- NHR regime accumulated **35,000+ beneficiaries** before closure to new applicants (Jan 2024). Replaced by NHR 2.0 / IFICI targeting innovation and science workers.

*Sources: AIMA / SEF data via The Portugal News; Plataforma Media; Portugal Homes; Wikipedia.*

### 6.2 Geographic Concentration

- **71.3% of foreign residents** in four coastal districts: Lisbon, Faro (Algarve), Setúbal, Porto.
- Algarve has highest foreign-resident density at **23.6% of inhabitants**.
- Lisbon: professionals, tech workers, digital nomads.
- Algarve: retirees (UK, Netherlands, Germany).
- Porto: lower-cost creative and entrepreneurial hub (growing).

*Sources: Algarve Addicts; Viv Europe; The Portugal News.*

### 6.3 Specific Insurance & Credit Needs

**Insurance needs by visa stage:**

| Stage | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Visa application (D7/D8) | Schengen-compliant travel insurance (min. €30,000) |
| AIMA residence appointment | Private health insurance covering hospitalization |
| Ongoing residence | Private health (faster access) + SNS access |
| Property purchase | Mandatory fire insurance (horizontal property); building insurance |
| Mortgage | Life insurance (effectively required by banks) |
| Vehicle | Motor insurance (requires NIF + Portuguese bank account) |

**Credit needs:**
- Mortgage access for non-residents: LTV typically **60–75%** (vs. 80–90% for residents), requiring 25–40% deposits.
- Processing: 8–12 weeks. Transaction costs ~7–8% of purchase price.
- Personal loans for non-residents: uncommon through standard retail banking.

### 6.4 Underserved Demand

- Only a handful of specialized brokers offer English-language insurance services (MEDAL, C1 Broker, Ibex Insurance, Feather).
- **No provider offers a unified "expat insurance journey"** covering travel → health → property → vehicle → life in a single platform.
- Cross-border complications (dual taxation, credit history portability, currency risk) receive limited advisory.
- Expats over 65 face health premiums exceeding **€900/month**.
- Digital nomads struggle with annual-contract structures mismatched to 1–2 year stays.
- AIMA backlog of ~400,000–900,000 pending cases compounds bureaucratic friction.

*Sources: Expatica; Portugal Pulse; Visas.pt; UCI; Global Citizen Solutions; Portugal Homes; Traverse International Finance.*

## 7. Implications for ReisierX

The data converges on a market defined by **high mandatory demand, low satisfaction, analog distribution, and rapid digital readiness**:

1. **SME insurance** — Workers' compensation alone is a €1.1B market, yet 74% of SMEs remain underprotected and 88% can't explain their professional liability coverage. The traditional intermediary network is contracting (−37% in 4 years) while surviving agents earn below minimum wage. This is a vacuum for a scalable, tech-enabled alternative.

2. **Credit intermediation momentum** — The explosive growth from 9% to 57% of mortgage volume in five years proves Portuguese consumers adopt intermediary-led financial services rapidly when they perceive value. The 94% irregularity rate among incumbents positions a compliant, digital-first entrant as a trust-based differentiator.

3. **WhatsApp-first distribution** — 97% smartphone penetration, ~90% WhatsApp usage, 6.5M MB WAY users. The infrastructure is there. Online insurance sales <1% and EIOPA's "face-to-face tradition" note define the gap, not an immovable barrier.

4. **Expat segment** — 1.54M foreign residents growing at ~18% annually, concentrated in four districts, with acute unmet needs and willingness to pay for English-language, digitally accessible financial services. No incumbent owns this market.

5. **Regulatory tailwinds** — Tightening oversight of credit intermediaries and insurance distribution favors operators who are audit-ready, transparent, and digitally traceable from day one.
