## The Franchise Model: Soluções & Decisões (S&D / D&S)

The two shareholders acquired a **franchise from Soluções & Decisões**, a credit and insurance intermediation franchise operator in Portugal.

**Commercial terms:**
- Initial lump-sum payment to acquire the franchise.
- Ongoing royalty: **10% of each transaction**.

**What the franchise provides:**
- Brand recognition and credibility.
- A "startup playbook" — structured onboarding into the business (processes, compliance setup).
- CRM-style tooling for managing intermediation processes.
- Pre-established **contacts and contracts with banks and insurers** — plug-and-play access that would otherwise require years of relationship-building. A significant door-opener.

**What the franchise does NOT restrict:**
- Both shareholders are **individually registered with ASF** — the franchise does not prevent direct ASF registration or require exclusivity.
- The franchise relationship is commercial (distribution/brand), not regulatory.

## Key Findings

### 1. Process is manual and product/provider-centric
Confirmed end-to-end. No intelligence layer. Operators navigate products and providers manually, without tools to match client need to optimal product/provider.

### 2. Real estate as the trigger event
Axado → credit + insurance intermediation. Same pattern as Rolando. Real estate transaction creates the credit moment, which creates the insurance moment. The trigger is structural, not coincidental.

### 3. Franchisor pain: managing agents
One of the S&D shareholders (franchisee, i.e., Tiago's group) said explicitly: **"I wish I could spend more time managing my agents"** — agents who work under the franchise.
- Time is consumed by process overhead, not relationship management or growth.
- This is a direct expression of the value prop: agent-resolv removes process friction so operators can focus on their network.

### 4. ASF mandatory training compliance = active friction
Keeping franchise agents compliant with ASF's mandatory ongoing training requirements is a pain point. This is:
- Regulatory (non-negotiable, has deadlines).
- Recurring (not a one-time event).
- Scalable pain (grows with agent count).
- **Product expansion angle:** Compliance tracking / training automation as a feature layer on top of core intermediation tooling.

## Strategic Implications

### ICP sharpens: Real estate + credit/insurance as the ICP pattern
Two independent data points (Rolando, Tiago) now confirm:
- Real estate is the upstream trigger.
- Credit + insurance intermediation is the value extraction layer.
- The operator is typically a separate entity or individual leveraging an existing real estate relationship network.
- Operators are under-tooled, process-heavy, and time-constrained.

### B2B2B2C angle: Franchisors as a wedge
S&D-type franchise operators manage **networks of agents** and are drowning in compliance and process overhead. A single contract with a franchisor could unlock many agents. This is a force-multiplier distribution path — worth raising with JP on product implications.

### The 10% royalty reveals incumbent value extraction without intelligence
S&D takes 10% per transaction in exchange for brand + playbook + pre-negotiated contracts. They are not providing intelligence, optimisation, or compliance automation. The gap is wide.

### Broker license acquisition (Bruno Mota angle)
Separately surfaced today (via Bruno Mota, met at Luís Santos's home): **buying an existing broker license** (one of the 68 active in Portugal) rather than applying from scratch. This connects to the S&D model — operators like S&D have licensing infrastructure that could be acquired or partnered with rather than replicated. Raise with Francisco Machado.
